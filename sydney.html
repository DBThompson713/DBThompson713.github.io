A picture book I saw in grade 2 changed my life.

I was born and raised in Northern Canada.

In grade two, we had to pick a country out of a big picture book of countries and write a small report on a major city.

My turn came, I flipped through the book.

Then I saw a strange building unlike anything I had ever seen before.

It looked so alien. Part hedgehog, part dishrack, part structure

I knew I needed to see it for myself. I promised myself I would. Time passed, but I never forgot.

It took two decades for me to set foot on Australian soil.

On my first day in Sydney, I walked to the Sydney Opera House from central station.
It was a beautiful sunny day, the walk was long.

It was incredible to see it in person. I never knew it was tiled. I placed my hands on the building that set me on this journey 20 years earlier.

I fell in love with Sydney, and knew I had to stay. That was 5 years ago, I'm now a permanent resident.

Sydney is my home.
